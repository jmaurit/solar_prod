A Bayesian multilevel logistic model of investment using Stan¶

Investing in hydropower plants: compliment or substitute to intermittent generation?¶

Why use Bayesian methods and Stan?¶

In short - you can relatively easily fit complicated, multilevel models while avoiding many of the traps of overfitting where you get a very good fit of the model to the data but get poor out-of-sample fit and poor predictive power (and in-turn valid inference).

I would also add, a flexibility and intuitiveness of dealing with a direct interpretation of probabilies and probability densities on parameters.

These issues are discussed fully in, for example:

Gelman and Hill

Kruschke

Gelman et al.

Stan is a robust, efficient and flexible probabilistic programming language: the Stan home page provides plenty of resources for other examples, documentation and explanation

Research topic and question¶

The research topic is to try to analyse the investment decision of hydropower plant owners who decide to upgrade their plants by installing new generators or upgrading existing generators. The dependent variable is then a 0/1 variable of whether or not an investment happens in a given year (2012-2014) for a particular plant. In particular, the question we want to pursue is what relation this investment decision has to the amount of renewable wind/solar energy that already exist in the area and how this interacts with a policy variable, which is whether there exists a Renewable Portfolio Standards that requires a certain percentage of renewables in the energy mix in the state where a hydroplant exists.

Hydropower - especially with a reservoir - can do a good job of balancing out the intermittency of solar or wind - that is the fact that you can not control when it is sunny or when it is windy. So a lot of solar and wind could lead to increased investment in hydropower plants. On the other hand, if owners are just trying to fill a quota - of which hydro counts, then we could expect a substitute effect where new hydro is installed instead of solar/wind.

The Stan Model¶












In [ ]:



    


 























In the stan code below, we have 4 levels in the model hierarchy: observation level which is a plant-year. Each plant-year is then grouped by plant (so every plant has three observations - 2012, 2013, 2014). In turn each plant belongs to a certain Transmission System Owner (TSO) - the entity that owns the power lines connecting the different power plants and the rest of the grid. We can expect a lot of variation here - some TSOs are vertically integrated utilities that also own the plants, other times power plant owners are seperate and private. Finally, I group the TSOs into two groups - those that operate in states with renewable portfolio standards and those that don't.


Starting at the model section of the stan code, we model the investment decision as a bernoulli model with logit link function. Going up a level to the transformed parameter section, the fitted values, y_hat are modelled as a random effect for each plant b_obs, a random effect for the report year, bry, and an error term epsilon, which is meant to capture over dispersion - that is an excess of zeros over what would be expected.


Going up a level in the transformed parameters sections, I model each plant-level random effect b_obs as an additive function of an intercept plus the effects of 5 plant-level variables: whether the plant has a reservoir, whether it uses pumped storage (can pump up water to a higher level at low demand times, in order to generate electricity at high demand) whether the power plant is a vertically integrated utility or an Independent Power Producer, the nameplate capacity of the plant and the year the plant started operation.


Again, moving up in the transformed parameters section, The coefficients on the plant level variables are modelled as an additive function of a coefficient on the percent of intermittents, mu_b of (wind and solar) in a TSO and a random effect. The mu_b variables are in turn modeled as having seperate distributions between RPS and non-RPS states. The posterior distributions of the mu_b coefficients are then what I am most interested in - comparing those in RPS and non-RPS states.



















Compared to the last model, a few changes to point out.


    

  • First, the hierarchy is now at the transmission system owner level. So each plant is organized as being a part of a TSO instead of a state. This gets over the issue of having renewable energy count towards a states RPS standard even if it is not in the state. Presumably, the plant has to be within a TSO that has a presence in the state. In turn, I split the TSO between those that have a presence in a RPS state and those that do not.
    

    • 

  • In this model I have also added indicator variables for the presence of a reservoir, pumped storage or whether the plant is a utiltiy or not.

pp_stan_model2 = """
data{
	int<lower = 0> N; // number of observations
	int<lower = 0> Y; //number or reporting years 2012-2014
	int<lower = 0> R; //RPS TSO (does TSO operate in at least one RPS state)
	int<lower = 0> S; //TSOs in data set
	int<lower = 0> P; //Number of individual power plants
	int<lower = 1, upper=3> report_year[N]; //normalized 2012-2014 to 1-3
	int<lower=0, upper=1432> plant[N];
	int<lower=0, upper=1> investment[N]; //no/yes investment made on plant

	vector[P] op_year; //first year of operation
	vector[P] nameplate; //nameplate capacity of plant
	vector[P] utility; //is utility (1) or IPP (0)
	vector[P] pumped_storage; //is pumped storage (1) or not(0)
	vector[P] isrsrvr; //is reservoir
    vector[P] plant_intermittent;
    int<lower=1, upper=48> state[P]; //State indicator of plant
	int<lower=1, upper=234> TSO[P]; //TSO indicator of plant
	//vector[S] wind_cap;
	//vector[S] solar_cap;
	vector[S] perc_intermittent;
	//vector[S] tot_nameplate;
	int<lower=1, upper=2> isRPS[S]; //indicator of whether TSO operates in RPS state 1= not RPS, 2= RPS
}

transformed data{
	real mean_nameplate;
	real<lower=0> sd_nameplate;
	vector[P] st_nameplate;
	real<lower=0, upper=1> mean_perc_intermittent;
	real<lower=0> sd_perc_intermittent;
    int<lower=0> K;
	vector[S] st_perc_intermittent;
    vector[P] age;
    vector[P] st_age;
    vector[P] st_utility;
    vector[P] st_pumped_storage;
    vector[P] st_isrsrvr;
    vector[P] st_intermittent;
    
    K<-5;

	mean_nameplate <- mean(nameplate);
	sd_nameplate <- sd(nameplate);
	st_nameplate <- (nameplate-mean_nameplate)/(2*sd_nameplate);

	mean_perc_intermittent <- mean(perc_intermittent);
	sd_perc_intermittent <- sd(perc_intermittent);
	st_perc_intermittent <- (perc_intermittent-mean_perc_intermittent)/(2*sd_perc_intermittent);
    
    //create age of plant paramter
    age <- (2012 - op_year);
    st_age <- (age - mean(age))/(2*sd(age));
    
    //plant level intermittent
    st_intermittent <- (plant_intermittent - mean(plant_intermittent))/(2*sd(plant_intermittent));
    
    //transform binary variables to have mean 0 and distance 1 between low/high
    st_utility <- utility - mean(utility);
    st_pumped_storage <- pumped_storage - mean(pumped_storage);
    st_isrsrvr <- isrsrvr - mean(isrsrvr);
}


parameters{
  real bry[Y]; //random effect for report year
  real<lower=0> sigma_bry; //sd of year effects
  real<lower=0> sigma_beta;
  real<lower=0> sigma_alpha;
  real<lower=0> sigma_re;
  real<lower=0> sigma_b;
  real<lower=0> sigma_a;
  real beta_a[R]; //
  real alpha_a[R]; //average effect for RPS or not. 
  matrix[K, R] alpha_b;
  matrix[K, R] beta_b; 
  real a_re[S]; //random effects term.
}


transformed parameters {
  real y_hat[N]; //individual means
  real b_obs[P];
  matrix[K, S] b; // matrix of coefficients
  real a[S]; //intercept term

  for (s in 1:S){
    for (k in 1:K){
      a[s] <- alpha_a[isRPS[s]] + beta_a[isRPS[s]]*st_perc_intermittent[s] + a_re[s];
      b[k, s] <- alpha_b[k, isRPS[s]] + beta_b[k, isRPS[s]]*st_perc_intermittent[s];
    }
  }

  for (p in 1:P){
    b_obs[p]<- a[TSO[p]] +
    b[1, TSO[p]]*st_isrsrvr[p] +
    b[2, TSO[p]]*st_pumped_storage[p] +
    b[3, TSO[p]]*st_utility[p] +
    b[4, TSO[p]]*st_nameplate[p] +
    b[5, TSO[p]]*st_age[p];
  }

  
  for (i in 1:N){
    y_hat[i] <- b_obs[plant[i]] + bry[report_year[i]];
  }
  
}

model{
  bry ~ cauchy(0, sigma_bry);//vectorized, Y
  sigma_bry ~  cauchy(0, 5); //
  sigma_beta ~ cauchy(0, 5); //variance parameter on distribution of beta, half-cauchy
  sigma_alpha ~ cauchy(0, 5); // variance parameter on distribution of alpha, half-cauchy
  sigma_b ~ cauchy(0, 5);
  sigma_a ~ cauchy(0, 5);
  to_vector(alpha_a) ~ cauchy(0, sigma_a); //vectorized, R
  to_vector(beta_a) ~ cauchy(0, sigma_b); //vectorized, R
  to_vector(alpha_b) ~ cauchy(0, sigma_a);
  to_vector(beta_b) ~ cauchy(0, sigma_b);
  to_vector(a_re) ~ cauchy(0, sigma_re); // random effets term

  investment ~ bernoulli_logit(y_hat); //vectorized, n
    
}

"""

def make_table(params, param_names):
    sum_table = [["param", "var", "2.5", "15", "50", "85", "97.5"]]
    for  p, param_df in enumerate(params):
        for i in param_df:
            row = [param_names[p], i]
            percentiles = get_percentiles(param_df[i]).tolist()
            for j in percentiles:
                row.append(round(j,3))
            sum_table.append(row)
    sum_table = pd.DataFrame(sum_table[1:], columns=sum_table[0])
    sum_table.set_index(["param", "var"], inplace=True)
    return(sum_table)

    st_age <- (age - mean(age))/(2*sd(age));
    
    //plant level intermittent
    st_intermittent <- (plant_intermittent - mean(plant_intermittent))/(2*sd(plant_intermittent));
    
    //transform binary variables to have mean 0 and distance 1 between low/high
    st_utility <- utility - mean(utility);
    st_pumped_storage <- pumped_storage - mean(pumped_storage);
    st_isrsrvr <- isrsrvr - mean(isrsrvr);
}

pp_stan_model4 = """
data{
	int<lower = 0> N; // number of observations
	int<lower = 0> Y; //number or reporting years 2012-2014
	int<lower = 0> R; //RPS TSO (does TSO operate in at least one RPS state)
	int<lower = 0> S; //TSOs in data set
	int<lower = 0> P; //Number of individual power plants
	int<lower = 0> K; //number of plant-level co-variates
    int<lower = 0> ST; //number of states

	int<lower = 1, upper=3> report_year[N]; //normalized 2012-2014 to 1-3
	int<lower=0, upper=1432> plant[N];
	int<lower=0, upper=1> investment[N]; //no/yes investment made on plant

	vector[P] op_year; //first year of operation
	vector[P] nameplate; //nameplate capacity of plant
	vector[P] utility; //is utility (1) or IPP (0)
	vector[P] pumped_storage; //is pumped storage (1) or not(0)
	vector[P] isrsrvr; //is reservoir
    vector[P] plant_intermittent; //plant level intermittent
    vector[P] isRPS_plant; //plant level intermittent
    int<lower=1, upper=48> state[P]; //State indicator of plant
	int<lower=1, upper=234> TSO[P]; //TSO indicator of plant
	//vector[S] wind_cap;
	//vector[S] solar_cap;
	vector[S] perc_intermittent;
	//vector[S] tot_nameplate;
	int<lower=1, upper=2> isRPS[S]; //indicator of whether TSO operates in RPS state 1= not RPS, 2= RPS
}

transformed data{
	real mean_nameplate;
	real<lower=0> sd_nameplate;
	vector[P] st_nameplate;
	real<lower=0, upper=1> mean_perc_intermittent;
	real<lower=0> sd_perc_intermittent;
	vector[S] st_perc_intermittent;
    vector[P] age;
    vector[P] st_age;
    vector[P] st_utility;
    vector[P] st_pumped_storage;
    vector[P] st_isrsrvr;
    vector[P] st_intermittent;
    vector[P] st_isRPS;

	mean_nameplate <- mean(nameplate);
	sd_nameplate <- sd(nameplate);
	st_nameplate <- (nameplate-mean_nameplate)/(2*sd_nameplate);

	mean_perc_intermittent <- mean(perc_intermittent);
	sd_perc_intermittent <- sd(perc_intermittent);
	st_perc_intermittent <- (perc_intermittent-mean_perc_intermittent)/(2*sd_perc_intermittent);
    
    //plant level intermittent
    st_intermittent <- (plant_intermittent - mean(plant_intermittent))/(2*sd(plant_intermittent));
    
    //plant level 
    st_isRPS <- (isRPS_plant - mean(isRPS_plant));
    
    //create age of plant paramter
    age <- (2012 - op_year);
    st_age <- (age - mean(age))/(2*sd(age));
    
    //transform binary variables to have mean 0 and distance 1 between low/high
    st_utility <- utility - mean(utility);
    st_pumped_storage <- pumped_storage - mean(pumped_storage);
    st_isrsrvr <- isrsrvr - mean(isrsrvr);
}


parameters{
    real b_mu[K];
    real<lower=0> b_sigma[K];
    //row_vector[K] b_p[ST]; //row_vector of coefficients
    matrix[K, ST] b_p; // matrix of coefficients
    real bry[Y];
    real meta_mu;
    real<lower=0> meta_sigma;
}


transformed parameters {
	real y_hat[N]; //individual means
	real b_obs[P];
	
    
	for (p in 1:P){
		b_obs[p]<- b_p[1, state[p]] +
        b_p[2, state[p]]*st_pumped_storage[p] + 
		b_p[3, state[p]]*st_isrsrvr[p] +
		b_p[4, state[p]]*st_utility[p] +
        b_p[5, state[p]]*st_nameplate[p] +
        b_p[6, state[p]]*st_age[p] +
        b_p[7, state[p]]*st_intermittent[p] +
        b_p[8, state[p]]*st_isRPS[p] +
        b_p[9, state[p]]*st_isRPS[p]*st_intermittent[p] +
        b_p[10, state[p]]*st_isRPS[p]*st_isrsrvr[p] +
        b_p[11, state[p]]*st_isRPS[p]*st_pumped_storage[p];
	}

	
	for (i in 1:N){
		y_hat[i] <- b_obs[plant[i]] + bry[report_year[i]];
	}
	
}

model{
	bry ~ cauchy(0, 10); //vectorized, Y
    for (k in 1:K){
        to_vector(b_p[k]) ~ cauchy(b_mu[k], b_sigma[k]);
        }
	//to_vector(b_p) ~ cauchy(b_mu, b_sigma); //vectorized
    to_vector(b_mu) ~ cauchy(0, meta_mu);
    to_vector(b_sigma) ~ cauchy(0, meta_sigma);
    meta_sigma ~ cauchy(0, 5);
    meta_mu ~ cauchy(0,2.5);
    investment ~ bernoulli_logit(y_hat); //vectorized, n
    
}
